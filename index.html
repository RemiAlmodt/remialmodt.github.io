Europe's Populists

European right-wing parties, movements and news sites on social media.

bootstrap themes
European right-wing actors understand how to use online media to their advantage. Especially the new media have enabled parties, groups or individuals to spread their ideas, opinions or ideology to a rather large audience, especially when it comes to the social media.

A network of actors from political and public sphere has emerged, providing mutual support for one another. Parties are spreading ideology, not only in the parliaments but also in the public sphere, where they are supported by civil movements. A thrid component in this right-wing network is marked by a vast number of alternative news media, which emerged in the wake of these parties and movements. These media organizations enforce the opinions shared by parties and movements and provide the suitably framed information for their audiences.

The international comparison shows that national and international events can be set into the context of publishing activities of the actors. For example, publishing increases during the refugee movements in the end of 2015, as well as during the UK referendum, also known as Brexit referendum in 2016, but also in the wake of the United Nations Compact for Migration, the so called migration pact, which was heavily criticised by right-wing populists from all over Europe.

Furthermore, we can observe increased publishing activities during national elections, such as France, during the presidential elections, or general elections in Germany or Austria. Publishing peaks can also be observed in other countries during that period. 

German right-wing opposition

Germany's AfD is currently one of the strongest opposition parties, since the 2017 election. The party was only founded in 2013 and made the jumo into most regional parliaments and the national one since then.

Compact, Germany's largest right-wing publisher

Compact magazine provides the German right-wing with suitably framed information. Criminal migrants and refugees, the radical Islam and political elites dominate their agenda and reporting. The publication openly supports AfD and other right-wing actors in the national and European political landscape.

PEGIDA, the movement

PEGIDA's name already suggests a strong connection to Christianity, opposing the migration from islamic countries mostly. The movement emerged in 2015 the wake of a large refugee influx into Germany and chancellor Merkel's open borders policy. The movement has been reported to have strong ties to AfD and other right-wing extremist actors.

Austria's governing populists

Since the party's foundation in the 1970's, Austria's right-wing FPÖ managed to enter in a governing coalition with conservative ÖVP, lasting until June 2019. The coalition failed over corruption allegations over their chairman Heinz-Christian Strache.

Wochenblick, the right-wing news outlet

Wochenblick is a publication with a target audience in the online media. The publication features articles about immigration, radical Islam, refugees and criticism over the European Union and its influence on Austrian domestic politics and policy. 

PEGIDA Austria, a franchise movement

Just as in the case of Germany, also Austria has a similar movement, called PEGIDA, which was founded after the German example. Topicality is similar, strong references to Christian values and criticism over refugee, asylum and migration policies on a national and European level.

SVP, for a sovereign Switzerland

SVP marks an example for several populist parties in Europe. Some argue that contemporary right-wing populism was created after the Swiss example. The party pushes a "Switzerland first" agenda, based on anti-EU and anti-migration narratives

Weltwoche, where politicians are editors

The Weltwoche news outlet has strong ties to right-wing populist SVP, as Roger Köppel is the main editor of the publication, who is also a politician for SVP. Such as the party, the publication featres articles about Swiss national sovereignty being in danger by immigration, multiculturalism and the European Union.

AUNS, Campaign for an Independent and Neutral Switzerland

As its name suggests, the AUNS movement shows a similar focus on a strong and independet Switzerland, surrounded by European Union member states. The movement emphasizes national sovereignty with a similar narrative and agenda as SVP and Weltwoche.

Hungary and its Fidesz government

The Hungarian Fidesz is one of the most succesful right-wing populist party on the European political landscape, holding a majority in the parliament and pushing for anti-Islam and anti-immigration policies for a long time now.

Hungarian Times, a Fidesz newspaper

The Hungarian Times labels itself as a conservative publication but has strong ties to Viktor Orban's Fidesz party. The publication is owned by party stakeholders an pushes a similar agenda like the Fidesz, based on a narrative, focusing on international elites, radical Islam and the negativity of immigration.

Generation Identity in Hungary

Initially, Generation Identity was founded in France but became a popular and modern right-wing movement in several European countries. The groups use the new media mostly to promote their action-activism and push a national identity and cultural heritage agenda, strongly connected to racial aspects.

UKIP, the brexit pioneers

The UKIP and its former leader, Nigel Farage, were some of the main supporters behind the Leave.EU campaign and the brexit, which eventually followed. The party shows a strong focus on an anti-EU, anti-Islam and anti-immigration narrative.

Westmonster for the Westminster

As the name suggests, the Westmonster news outlet deals with corrupt elites, not only in London but in Brussels as well. The publication has strong ties to the UKIP and the Leave.EU campaign, topic wise but also personel wise.

Leave.EU, the brexiteer campaign

The Leave.EU campaign was founded in the wake of the UK referendum in 2016. The movement consists of politicians from conservative and right-wing populist parties and has strong ties to the Westmonster news outlet. Also on a topic level, the movement focuses on the European Union, migration and the radical Islam.

Rassemblement National, the former FN

The party of Marine Le Pen, daughter of its founder Jean-Marie Le Pen, is one of the oldest right-wing parties in Europe. Initially an extreme right-wing party, it shifted towards a more populist and centerd approach in the past 15 years.

Fdesouche, a mockery that became news

Initially a page for memes about left-wing and conservative politicians, Fdesouche became a news outlet with a strong publishing activity on the social media. The site shows strong personal ties to the RN and the topics pushed by it, such as the European Union and immigration.

Europe of Nations and Freedom, a movement for Europes right-wingers

The ENF started as a movement, pushing the right-wing populist agenda of sovereign nation states, as opposed to the ideals of the European Union and is now a pool of right-wing actors from all over Europe with a common agenda and narrative.
